April 6, 2015 Meeting Notes

  • Met with Professor Dietz and Haden. Presented current version of OCR, and where we are at the moment with the shape detection.
  • Ideas proposed by Dietz:
    1. Instead of pure shape detection, go searching vertical/horizontal lines and if you see the color white then there is a good possibility that there is going to be some text inside of the field.
    2. Check for if something is surrounded. (possible problems exist however with this approach).
    3. Multi-resolution segmentation. Take image, scale it down, do recognition on that, then next recognition at a higher resolution should be consistent. If you scale the resolution down then the text basically disappears so all you have is your box/shapes.
    4. Recommends something more simple than opencv overall. Opencv apparently is too complicated for what we are working towards.

  • What customer wants: just simple segmentation and OCR within segments. Nothing too fancy, so no need to focus on unique shapes, focus only on rectangles/squares for the time being.
